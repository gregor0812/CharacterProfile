Nobody returns home the same as when they first left their doorstep.
For some, even returning back home in one piece is a luxury.
This strange thing called war is waged for reasons only the dead can dare judge. It has a way of obscuring humanity like a dreadful fog; it turns lives into statistics, weapons into logistics and victory a price measured in blood.

For the soldiers themselves, it is individuality that is first surrendered to make way for survival instinct. This exchange for the ability to suppress common sense, to do battle and take life without hesitation. Not unlike animals, the taste of blood can never be forgotten and the changes not undone.

When the war was over and swords were turned to ploughshares, true farewells were spoken. The few that made it back returned as men, no longer the boys they once were. Their youth destroyed and the void filled the challenges of adulthood. They had been taught how to defend their bodies but not their minds, haunted by the demand for constant vigilance against dangers that no longer were.

With every passing step Jia saw what could have been in the village; An ageless illusion he had to say goodbye to. But when he closed his eyes, he would remember his friends that weren’t here. Instead, their coffins that held the their lifeless bodies. They were gone within mere moments, and the world would have to turn without them. A myriad of possibilities for who would have been heroes in their lifetime. None were theirs now.
Too quietly, a generation of candles bowed to the darkness, little flames swallowed by black."

Hooks

Jia Zhang was conscripted by Garlemald and fought in many of the Empire's campaigns.

If you are an easterner, it is easy to go against Jia's grains by virtue of his participation in the Doman war

Philosophy is a hobby of him that he is willing to eagerly engage in.

Jia is contracted by the Thalian Circle, a group of Sharlayan radicals who seek to contain artifacts and knowledge that were exposed when Idyllshire was abandoned.

Trivia

Jia often mistakes his own volume, and talks louder than he intends to.

He never properly recorded his date of birth, and he ties his years to the Equinox

Language is something Jia struggles with. While there is nothing wrong with his syntax, his tribal background create remarkable choice of words.

The reason Jia often has pieces of clothing and fabric that belonged to other people is that their scents allow him to more easily recall and remember people.

Face

Hair: His ashen hair varies on length depending on the long intervals between cuts. His tuft of hair that usually reaches past his ears is surprisingly dense and soft to the touch.

Structure: His facial structure is angular and borders gaunt with high cheekbones. Jia's smooth complexion is marred by a scar that runs from his right horn to his chin, chipping his scales.

Eyes: With eyes that all too often is mistaken for a negative disposition, Jia has sharp eyes with a verdant green iris surrounded by a golden limbus.

Body

Height: Typical for Au'ra he often stands out due to his height, reaching 6'11 in total. Even with his considerable bulk, Jia tends to look lanky.

Physique: Jia hails from a life of labour on the steppes resulting in a brawny physique tempered by martial experience.

Skin: His skin is slightly tanned and riddled with alabaster scars. Most of these unremarkable, notching themselves along his hand, forearms and legs. A select few however are more than a couple inches long, prominently the scar on his face. The longest being a foot long marking across his upper back

Clothes

Armour: Jia's battle garb consists of an armoured surcoat that protects his vitals while remaining light overall.

Weapon: The Magitek gunblade on his back is reminiscent of Garlean make and similar to the modern gunblades used by the Empire

Clothing: His luxurious regalia is often considered gaudy and a poor man's idea of a rich man's clothes to those of true upper class.
